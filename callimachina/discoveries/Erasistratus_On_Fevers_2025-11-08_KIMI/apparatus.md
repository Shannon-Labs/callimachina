# Critical Apparatus: Erasistratus - On Fevers

## Fragmentary Sources

### Galen, On Different Types of Fever
**Date**: c. 180 CE  
**Type**: Medical citation and critique  
**Confidence**: 0.90  
**Content**: Extensive references to Erasistratus' fever theory

**Nature of Citations**: Galen frequently discusses Erasistratus' fever theory, both to preserve his ideas and to critique them from his own humoral perspective. His citations are detailed and technical.

**Specific Theories Preserved**:
1. **Pneumatic theory**: Fever as obstruction of pneuma flow
2. **Mechanistic explanation**: Friction generating heat
3. **Classification system**: Quotidian, tertian, quartan fevers
4. **Treatment approaches**: Dietary management, timing
5. **Diagnostic methods**: Pulse, observation, timing

**Reliability**: High. Galen is a careful medical historian who preserves earlier theories accurately even when disagreeing with them.

**Technical Details**: Galen preserves Erasistratus' precise medical terminology and theoretical framework.

## Historical Context

### Erasistratus' Medical Theory

**Pneumatic Physiology**: Erasistratus developed comprehensive theory:
- **Pneuma**: Vital spirit flowing through body
- **Vessels**: Veins, arteries, nerves as conduits
- **Obstruction**: Disease as blockage of flow
- **Friction**: Heat generated by blocked flow
- **Inflammation**: Primary pathological process

**Revolutionary Aspects**:
- **Mechanistic**: Physical explanations, not humors
- **Systematic**: Comprehensive theoretical framework
- **Empirical**: Based on observation and logic
- **Predictive**: Could explain various phenomena

**Historical Significance**:
- **Alternative to humoral theory**: Different paradigm
- **Influenced later medicine**: Galen's synthesis
- **Scientific methodology**: Logical deduction from observations
- **Therapeutic implications**: Diet, timing, environment

### Alexandrian Medical Innovation

**Context**: Erasistratus worked in Alexandria (c. 315-240 BCE):
- **Royal patronage**: Ptolemaic support for research
- **Research environment**: Systematic investigation
- **Human dissection**: Following Herophilus' precedent
- **International**: Attracted students from Mediterranean

**Relationship to Herophilus**:
- **Contemporary**: Overlapping careers
- **Rival schools**: Different theoretical approaches
- **Common method**: Both used dissection
- **Complementary**: Anatomy (Herophilus) + physiology (Erasistratus)

### Later Medical Tradition
