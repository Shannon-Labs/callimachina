CRITICAL APPARATUS:
The Protrepticus (Exhortation to Philosophy) represents Aristotle's early Platonic period before his mature system. It argues that true happiness comes through contemplative wisdom rather than wealth, pleasure, or honor. Iamblichus preserves extensive fragments, and Cicero's adaptation confirms the work's structure and protreptic effectiveness. The work was highly influential in antiquity, particularly through Cicero's Hortensius, which profoundly influenced Augustine.
