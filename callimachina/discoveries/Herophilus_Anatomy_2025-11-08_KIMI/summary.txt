CRITICAL APPARATUS:
Herophilus was the "father of anatomy" who first systematically dissected human cadavers in Alexandria, making revolutionary discoveries. He identified the brain as the governing organ (not just for cooling), distinguished nerves from tendons, described the four ventricles of the brain, and discovered the ovaries. His work established anatomy as a scientific discipline and created systematic methods for anatomical investigation. His influence on subsequent medicine was profound, particularly through Galen who frequently cites him as an authority.

SCHOLARLY SIGNIFICANCE:

His systematic dissection methods and detailed observations created the foundation for all subsequent medical knowledge. The discovery of the brain's true function, nerve-tendon distinction, and female reproductive anatomy represented quantum leaps in understanding. His work influenced medicine for over 2,200 years, from Galen through the Renaissance to modern medicine. Herophilus represents the beginning of empirical medical science, establishing methods of dissection, observation, and documentation that remain fundamental to medicine today.
