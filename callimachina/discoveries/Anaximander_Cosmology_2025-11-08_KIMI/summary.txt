CRITICAL APPARATUS:
Anaximander's Cosmology represents the first systematic philosophical treatise in Western thought, introducing revolutionary concepts that established Presocratic natural philosophy. The work presented the apeiron (boundless/infinite) as transcendent principle beyond elemental monism, developed systematic naturalistic cosmology rejecting mythological explanations, proposed revolutionary astronomical theories including Earth's free suspension in space, advanced evolutionary theory of life's natural origin from aquatic forms, explained meteorological phenomena through natural atmospheric processes, established cosmic justice through natural balance of opposing forces, and articulated cosmic perspective emphasizing human limitations in face of infinite reality. The work's influence on subsequent Presocratic philosophy was foundational, establishing paradigms for rational inquiry, naturalistic explanation, and systematic cosmological speculation.
