## Fragmentary Sources

**Date**: c. 320 BCE  
**Type**: Systematic doxographical compilation  
**Confidence**: 0.95  
**Content**: Primary source for Anaximandrian cosmology

**Nature of Compilation**: Theophrastus, Aristotle's successor, compiled systematic accounts of Presocratic philosophy, providing the most reliable source for Anaximander's cosmological theories.

**Specific Content Preserved**:
1. **Apeiron doctrine**: Complete account of boundless principle
2. **Cosmic generation**: Theory of world formation
3. **Celestial mechanics**: Explanation of heavenly bodies
4. **Terrestrial phenomena**: Earth position and structure
5. **Biological theories**: Evolutionary development of life

**Reliability**: Exceptionally high. Theophrastus wrote within two centuries of Anaximander and had access to his complete works.
