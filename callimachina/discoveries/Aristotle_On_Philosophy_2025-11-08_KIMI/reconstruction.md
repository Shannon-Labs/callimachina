## Book 2: The Parts of Philosophy

### Theoretical Philosophy

Theoretical philosophy concerns itself with truth for its own sake […] It includes theology, mathematics, and natural philosophy [Philo]. Each studies a different aspect of reality, but all aim at understanding [?].

### Practical Philosophy

[…] Practical philosophy aims at action and the good life [Cicero]. It includes ethics, politics, and economics, all directed toward human flourishing [?].

### The Unity of Philosophy

Despite these divisions, philosophy is one […] All parts are connected, like members of a body, each contributing to the whole [Clement].
