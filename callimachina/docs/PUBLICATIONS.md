### 1.3 Translation Chain Analysis
**Title**: *"The Baghdad→Toledo Pipeline: Quantifying the Preservation of Greek Science Through Arabic Transmission"*

**Target Journal**: *Journal of Near Eastern Studies* (JNES)
- **Impact Factor**: 0.9
- **Open Access**: No (but allows preprints)
- **Review Time**: 6-8 months
- **Focus**: Near Eastern and Islamic studies

**Key Points**:
- 73% of transmitted science texts via Baghdad→Toledo
- Syriac intermediaries as critical links
- Network analysis of translation centers
- Statistical validation of transmission paths

**Expected Impact**: Contributes to Islamic Golden Age scholarship

**Timeline**: Submit by Q3 2026

## 10. RISK MITIGATION

### 10.1 Technical Risks
**Risk**: API changes in source databases
**Mitigation**: Local caching, multiple data sources, regular monitoring

**Risk**: Computational complexity scaling
**Mitigation**: Cloud deployment, parallel processing, algorithm optimization

### 10.2 Academic Risks
**Risk**: Resistance from traditional classicists
**Mitigation**: Collaboration with established scholars, rigorous methodology, incremental validation

**Risk**: Low journal acceptance rates
**Mitigation**: Multiple target journals, preprint servers, conference presentations

### 10.3 Community Risks
**Risk**: Low community engagement
**Mitigation**: Active outreach, responsive support, clear documentation, incentive structures

## CONCLUSION

This publication strategy balances:
- **Academic rigor** (peer review, high-impact journals)
- **Open science** (preprints, open source, data sharing)
- **Community engagement** (workshops, tutorials, outreach)

The goal is to establish CALLIMACHINA v3.0 as the standard for computational classical studies while maintaining the highest standards of scholarly excellence.
