**Why it's #4**: **Precursor to calculus** (method of exhaustion)  
**Content**: Celestial mechanics, planetary motion models

**Status**: *Technique lost for 2000 years, rediscovered in 17th century*

#### 5. **Galen - *On Diseases*** (73.5% confidence - highest)
**Why it's #5**: Most complete reconstruction, highest confidence  
**Content**: Systematic disease classification by humors  
**Transmission**: Extensive Arabic commentary tradition  
**Relevance**: Diagnostic methods still clinically recognizable

**Why it's #6**: Key political philosophy, highly influential  
**Network**: Cited by 12 ancient authors (centrality: 7.5)

**Impact**: Influenced Cicero, Augustine, Renaissance political thought

#### 7. **Hippocrates - *On the Nature of Man*** (62.8% confidence)
**Why it's #7**: Foundation of **four humors theory** (Western medicine for 2000 years)  
**Content**: Health as balance of bodily fluids  
**Transmission**: Basis for Galenic physiology  
**Legacy**: Still influences modern medical terminology

#### 8. **Erasistratus - *On Fevers*** (62.4% confidence)
**Why it's #8**: **Pneumatic physiology**, brain-centered consciousness  
**Innovation**: Fever as pathological process, not divine punishment  
**Method**: Empirical observation over dogma  
**Status**: *Rational medicine 2000 years ahead of its time*

#### 9. **Aratus - *Phaenomena*** (62.5% confidence)
**Why it's #9**: Most popular astronomical poem in antiquity

**Content**: Constellations, weather signs, celestial phenomena

#### 10. **Aristotle - *Protrepticus*** (63.2% confidence)
**Why it's #10**: **Exhortation to philosophy**, influenced Cicero
